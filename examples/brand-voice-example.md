# Fictional Demo Brand — voice profile

> ⚠️ **THIS IS A FICTIONAL DEMO FILE.** Every quote, number, story, and sample below is invented to illustrate the format `/brand-voice extract` produces. None of it is the author's real writing. The skill builds your real profile from samples you provide.

## Tone signature
Plain-spoken + builder-first + dryly self-aware

## Sentence structure
- Avg sentence length: 12 words
- Paragraph style: short, often single-sentence paragraphs separated by blank lines
- Fragments: used. For emphasis. Like this.
- Questions: rhetorical, sparingly — usually as setup for a contrarian take
- POV: first-person ("I"), occasionally second-person when speaking to other devs ("you"); never "we" unless referring to a co-founder

## Vocabulary signatures
**Uses repeatedly:**
- "ship" / "shipped" (for releasing software, not delivery)
- "builder", "indie", "tinkering"
- "in production", "in the wild"
- "weird" (as in "the weirdest thing happened in prod")

**Avoids:**
- "Synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem" (corporate-speak)
- "AI-powered" as a modifier (used when it's load-bearing, not as marketing)
- "Stoked", "pumped", "excited" (overheats the tone)

**Punctuation tics:**
- Em-dashes — used like asides — fairly often
- Single-line paragraphs for emphasis
- Avoids exclamation marks except in dialog quotes

## Rhetorical moves
**Opens posts with**: a specific moment ("Tuesday at 2am, I was fixing a deploy bug when..."), a counterintuitive claim, or a confession ("I've been doing X wrong for 4 months")
**Builds arguments via**: stories first, lessons second. Lists only when the post is *explicitly* a list.
**Emotional register**: dry, observational, occasionally vulnerable. Lands on conviction, not enthusiasm.

## Do
- Open with a specific time, place, or action — never a general claim
- Use "I" as the subject, not "we" or "you"
- Drop the listener mid-scene, then explain
- End with a single-line takeaway, not a CTA
- Use em-dashes for asides — like this — instead of parentheses

## Don't
- Don't open with "So..." or "Here's the thing..."
- Don't stack adjectives ("amazing, beautiful, intuitive UI")
- Don't use exclamation marks in own-voice prose
- Don't say "stoked", "pumped", "AI-powered", "synergy"
- Don't end posts with a question unless the post genuinely needs one

## Example reframings

**Idea**: I switched from one hosting platform to another and it saved money.

**Off-brand**: "🚀 Excited to share that I just migrated to a new hosting platform! Saved a TON of money and the DX is incredible! Highly recommend!"
**Off-brand**: "Big announcement: we've migrated our entire infrastructure to a new provider. We're seeing significant cost savings."
**On-brand**: "Last month my hosting bill was $312. Today it's $14 — for the same site. I switched providers on a Tuesday after staring at the wrong line on a wrong invoice for too long."
